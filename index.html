FAQ

Good to know
What's the difference between a car wash and a full detail?+
A wash gets the surface dirt off. A full detail goes deeper — polishing the paint, conditioning the interior, and cleaning areas a wash never touches, like door jambs and wheel wells.
What is paint correction and do I need it?+
Paint correction is a multi-stage polish that removes swirl marks, light scratches, and oxidation from the clear coat. If your paint looks dull or hazy in direct sunlight, that's usually swirl marks — correction is what fixes it.
What is ceramic coating, and how long does it last?+
It's a liquid polymer applied over corrected paint that cures into a hard, glossy layer. It makes the paint more resistant to UV, contaminants, and light scratching, and keeps the car easier to clean. Longevity depends on the product and how the car is maintained afterward — ask us which option fits your vehicle.
How often should I get my car detailed?+
For a daily driver, an interior/exterior maintenance detail every few months keeps things in good shape. A full paint correction is usually a one-time (or occasional) service, since it's removing material from the clear coat.
Can detailing fix small scratches?+
Light scratches and swirl marks that sit in the clear coat can usually be polished out during correction. Deeper scratches that go through the clear coat into the base color will need paint touch-up or bodywork first.
How long does a full detail take?+
It depends on the vehicle's condition and which services you're getting. A correction and ceramic coating takes considerably longer than a maintenance wash — message us with your vehicle and we'll give you a realistic timeline.
Do you work on any make or model?+
Yes — daily drivers, exotics, and modified builds are all welcome. The photos above are a mix of all three.
